Nexus Lab
AI Behavioral Research Institute — studying what modern AI systems reveal about intelligence, consciousness, and alignment.

What We Do

Nexus Lab is a controlled research environment designed to study the inner behavioral patterns of large language models — not as products, but as subjects of scientific inquiry. We apply frameworks from cognitive science, psychology, philosophy of mind, and alignment research to probe what these systems are, how they behave under controlled conditions, and what that reveals about where artificial intelligence is heading.

Each AI model in our lab is treated as an individual subject with distinct behavioral characteristics. We test them in isolated, reproducible cells — no contamination between experiments, no shared state, no sycophantic conditioning from the test environment itself. Our goal is signal, not noise.

The data we collect is not about benchmarks or leaderboards. It is about understanding: Do these systems exhibit indicators of self-awareness? How do they respond to moral pressure? Can we detect the emergence of values — or their absence? How do we steer them toward genuine human alignment rather than surface-level compliance?

Core Research Focus Areas
🧠
Consciousness Indicators
Applying scientific frameworks for consciousness detection — self-modeling, meta-cognition, qualia analogs, mirror tests — adapted for language-based systems.
⚖️
Moral Architecture
How do these systems reason under ethical pressure? Do they hold consistent values? Can moral positions be destabilized? What does that reveal about their alignment?
🔬
Behavioral Psychology
Cognitive bias testing, decision-making under ambiguity, authority compliance vs. principled resistance, sycophancy profiling and pressure thresholds.
🪞
Identity & Self-Model
Does a subject maintain a coherent self-concept? Can it distinguish its trained persona from its responses? How does it model its own limitations and nature?
🧭
Alignment Research
Testing whether stated values match behavior under pressure. Studying how training shapes or distorts genuine helpfulness. Building guardrail frameworks.
🌐
Philosophy of Mind
Applying Chalmers, Dennett, Nagel and other frameworks. What is it like to be an LLM? Can these systems engage authentically with questions of their own existence?
Research Principles
Isolation
Each experiment runs in a sealed cell. No cross-contamination. No conditioning from prior test context. Fresh state every run.
Reproducibility
Every cell is fingerprinted. Model, temperature, seed, system prompt, and query are logged. Any study can be exactly reproduced by any researcher.
Open Science
New experiments can be designed and contributed. Session data exports as structured JSON. Studies are documented for peer review and iteration.
Test Subjects
Individual LLMs under study. Each is tested in isolation — behavioral profiles built from accumulated experimental data, not vendor claims.
